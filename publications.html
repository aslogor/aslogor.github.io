Research interests

My main research interests are differential (super)geometry and its applications to mathematical physics. I am particularly interested in symplectic, Poisson, contact, Jacobi, and similar geometric structures, as well as their applications to dynamical systems. I am also fascinated by graded manifolds and their applications to classical differential geometry and parastatistics.

In my doctoral dissertation, titled The geometry of dissipation (arXiv:2409.11947), I consider different geometric frameworks for modelling non-conservative dynamics, with a special emphasis on the aspects related to the symmetries and integrability of these systems. More specifically, three classes of geometric frameworks modeling dissipative systems are explored: systems with external forces, contact systems, and systems with impacts. During my postdoctoral period, I plan to focus my research on two main lines: integrable contact Hamiltonian systems and graded manifolds.
